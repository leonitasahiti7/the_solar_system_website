F.A.Q.

Frequently Asked Questions about our Solar System and the project.

As of 2006, the International Astronomical Union (IAU) reclassified Pluto as a "dwarf planet." It meets two of the three criteria for a planet: it orbits the Sun and has sufficient mass for a nearly round shape. However, it has not "cleared the neighborhood" around its orbit.

The Solar System is approximately 4.571 billion years old. This age is calculated by radiometric dating of meteorites.

Venus is the hottest planet in our solar system, with surface temperatures hot enough to melt lead (about 465°C / 870°F), due to its thick atmosphere which creates a runaway greenhouse effect.

Currently, Mars is hostile to life as we know it due to radiation, low pressure, and freezing temperatures. However, it is the most habitable candidate for future colonization, and many space agencies are working on technologies to make this possible.

Navigate to our "Gallery" page to view high-resolution images of all planets. You can also contact us for specific data requests via the Services page.
